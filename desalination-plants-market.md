<p><p>Desalination plants primarily cater to two market types: seawater and brackish water. Seawater desalination involves treating ocean water to produce freshwater, typically employing reverse osmosis or distillation methods. This market is significant in arid coastal regions. Brackish water desalination targets slightly saline water sources, such as estuaries or underground aquifers, which require less energy to treat than seawater. Both markets play crucial roles in addressing water scarcity, supporting agriculture, and ensuring sustainable water supply in various regions.</p></p>
