<p><p>The fruit and vegetable seeds market can be categorized into two main types: hybrid and open-pollinated seeds. Hybrid seeds are created through controlled pollination to produce plants with desirable traits, often resulting in higher yields and disease resistance. Open-pollinated seeds, on the other hand, come from naturally pollinated plants, allowing for seed saving and biodiversity. Each type serves different farmer needs, with hybrids appealing for commercial production and open-pollinated seeds valued for sustainability and traditional farming practices.</p></p>
