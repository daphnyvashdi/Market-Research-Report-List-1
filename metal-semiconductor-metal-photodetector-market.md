<p><p>The Metal-Semiconductor-Metal (MSM) photodetector market primarily consists of GaAs-based and InGaAs-based technologies. GaAs-based photodetectors leverage the high electron mobility and direct bandgap of gallium arsenide, making them suitable for high-frequency applications. In contrast, InGaAs-based detectors utilize indium gallium arsenide alloy, offering extended sensitivity in the infrared spectrum, particularly advantageous for telecommunications and sensing applications. Both types exhibit rapid response times and are essential in various fields such as data communication, spectroscopy, and environmental monitoring.</p></p>
