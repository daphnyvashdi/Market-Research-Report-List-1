<p><p>The generic injectable drugs market is divided into two primary types: small molecules and large molecules. Small molecule injectables consist of simple, low-cost compounds typically synthesized through chemical processes. They are widely used for various therapeutic applications and are easier to manufacture. In contrast, large molecule injectables refer to biologics, which are more complex, often derived from living organisms. These drugs, including monoclonal antibodies and vaccines, generally require sophisticated production methods and are used for specialized treatments.</p></p>
