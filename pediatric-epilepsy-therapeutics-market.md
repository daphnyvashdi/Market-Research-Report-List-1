<p><p>The pediatric epilepsy therapeutics market encompasses various epilepsy types, including Benign Rolandic Epilepsy, characterized by seizures during sleep and usually resolves in adolescence. Childhood Absence Epilepsy features brief, frequent absence seizures. Juvenile Myoclonic Epilepsy is marked by myoclonic jerks and generalized seizures, typically starting in adolescence. Infantile Spasms are severe seizures occurring in infants, often linked to developmental issues. Lennox-Gastaut Syndrome presents multiple seizure types, significant cognitive impairments, and requires comprehensive therapeutic approaches for management.</p></p>
